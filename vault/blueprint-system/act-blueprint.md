# Act Blueprint

## Purpose

An act holds the rules that repeat across many scenes — motifs, world language, recurring behavior — so no individual scene has to define them on its own.

## Content

**Act Purpose** — what this act needs to establish, emotionally and structurally.

**World / Design Language** — the visual and tonal rules that apply throughout, stated once.

**Core Motifs** — recurring images and what they mean at this stage, so later scenes can use them without redefining them.

**Scene Map** — a list of the act's scenes and what each one is for, not what happens inside them in detail.

## Rule

Nothing that belongs at the act level gets repeated inside a scene blueprint unless that scene uses it differently. If a scene needs to restate a global rule to make sense, the rule wasn't placed high enough.
