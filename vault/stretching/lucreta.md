```text
                             NOEME

                            LUCRETA

                           written by

                         Diogo Oliveira

INT. LUCRETA'S BEDROOM — DAY

                           PROLOGUE.

Lucreta sits quietly on the corner of her bed. The room dims
with passing clouds through the steel-framed window. VERA
arranges a headdress of white orchids and rosemary, carefully
weaving them into Lucreta's crimson hair.

Lucreta's fingers trace the ornate mahogany at the foot of the
bed, following the intricate curves carved into the wood from
where they begin to where they end.

Her hand stops.

Her ear turns toward the closed window.

Beyond it, carriage wheels roll across stone. Voices follow.

She moves her hand to her waist. She stands and gently pushes
Vera aside. Her stride stiffens, but it is barely noticed.

Vera speaks no words, instead dropping her head as Lucreta
passes by.

The outside brims with life — carriages rolling across cobbled
streets, the suits and dresses of passing lives, wooden crates
stacked with bottles, baskets overflowing with fruit,
parasols, hats, bags, canes.

Lucreta keeps looking as Vera gently pulls her away from the
window. The street slips from view. Her eyes lift. Above the
rooftops, a bright blue sky.

She closes her eyes.

A gentle knock stirs the silence, though the voice that
follows carries far less patience.

                           VOICE (O.S.)

                      Lucreta! Are you ready yet? The
                      guests have been waiting nearly an
                      hour already. Surely you do not
                      intend to leave your groom
                      languishing from boredom before he
                      can even marry you.

When she opens her eyes again, her pale-mint eyes anchor her
reflection, erasing everything else. Slowly, the rest of her
comes into focus around them — the ivory folds of her wedding
gown blooming toward the floor, lace contours contrasting the
vivid colors her eyes have lost.

Vera's gaze lingers upon the reflection for only a moment
before falling away. Her hands return to the delicate strands
cascading over Lucreta's shoulders.

                           VERA

                      Do not tell me you have grown
                      fearful of womanhood at the final
                      hour, dear. You linger so long one
                      might think this marriage burdens
                      you terribly.

The room settles into silence. Lucreta's gaze remains fixed.

                           LUCRETA

                      No, Vera. Quite the contrary.

Nothing could—

The words break apart beneath a sudden spasm in her chest. She
turns sharply, one hand catching the edge of the table as the
cough tears free from her throat. For a moment she cannot
breathe.

When it passes, she draws in a slow, careful breath.

                           LUCRETA (CONT'D)

                      Nothing could mar this day. There
                      is nothing I have ever desired more
                      than this hour.

The cough returns before the final word has fully left her
mouth. Sharper this time. A faint stain blooms against her
lips.

Her eyes widen. A hand rises quickly to her mouth.
She wipes the blood away before turning her palm against the
fabric of her gown.
Only then remembering what she is wearing.

Vera's hands still.

For the briefest moment, worry surfaces across her reflection.
Then something practiced settles over it instead. A careful
smile, gentle enough to preserve the moment rather than
shatter it.

                           VERA

                      So— how long before I may tend to
                      the child you are certain to bless
                      us with?

A subtle tilt of Lucreta's chin.

                           LUCRETA

                      And yet you speak of that child so
                      readily. As though you have spent
                      longer preparing for its coming
                      than my own mother ever spent
                      preparing for me.

Vera's smile weakens briefly, taking much of the room's
fragile warmth with it. Lucreta watches her.

A knock bursts through the moment.

                           VOICE (O.S.)

                      Lucreta! Your father will grow
                      tired of the groom before you are
                      even married. Come out, girl — at
                      this rate you shall emerge old and
                      wrinkled before the ceremony
                      begins.

Vera slides a hand across Lucreta's shoulder and down her arm
before stepping away.

                           VERA

                      Go, dear. No one deserves this
                      moment more than you.

A tear slips down her cheek. She wipes it away before it can
fall any further, turning slightly to hide her reflection from
view.

Lucreta straightens slowly and gathers the skirts of her gown
with practiced grace. A blur halts her step. Again. She
continues toward the door.

From the corner of her eye, a faint trail of her reflection
lingers a fraction too long — as though it too has faltered.

Lucreta's hand closes around the handle.

Silence.

                           VOICE (O.S.)

                      L—ta!

Her fingers loosen, sliding along polished brass.

                           VOICE (O.S.)

                      —creta!

The room lurches sideways. A violent knock tears through
everything else, swallowing her senses in a single sharp
impact of sound.

Her pale-mint eyes remain fixed and hollow as her body
crumples across the floor.

END OF PROLOGUE — NOEME
```
