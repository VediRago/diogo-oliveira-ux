# Layered Narrative System

Fragments is built in layers.

The surface story follows characters.

The deeper structure connects fragments underneath that surface.

## Layer 1 — Character Story

This is the surface layer.

It contains relationships, loss, survival, attachment, and conflict.

Lucreta, Phil, Abigail, Sera, Kael, and Erit have to work as people before they work as parts of a larger system.

## Layer 2 — Structural Story

This layer connects fragments.

It uses recurring images, symptoms, visual motifs, outcomes, displacement, continuity, and Bridge System elements.

These patterns are evidence, not exposition.

They create recognition without stopping the story to explain itself.

## Layer 3 — Interpretation Story

This layer belongs to the reader.

Readers build theories from the evidence available to them.

Those theories can work before the full structure is visible.

Stretching lets those theories expand instead of being discarded.

## Layer 4 — Hidden System

This layer keeps the narrative coherent.

It does not need to be explained directly.

It gives the connections between fragments a reason to exist.

## Summary

Fragments operates through four layers:

> Character Story
> Structural Story
> Interpretation Story
> Hidden System

Each layer does a different job.
