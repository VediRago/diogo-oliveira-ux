# Scene — Bedroom Preparation

Status:
- Scene blueprint
- Prose exists
- Adaptation/script version not written

Act:
- Act 01 — Lucreta

Characters:
- Lucreta
- Vera

Location:
- Lucreta's bedroom / ceremonial preparation room
- Window overlooking ordinary civilian life outside

Purpose:
- Introduce Lucreta through withholding, ritual, and contrast.
- Show that what pulls her toward the window is not only sky, but ordinary life outside.
- Delay the face reveal until the mirror shot.

Scene Summary:
The scene opens behind Lucreta as Vera places the flower crown. Lucreta sits near the foot of the bed with her head lowered, her face withheld. Her attention drifts to the carved wood of the bedframe, her fingers tracing the grooves as the camera slowly moves closer.

A sound from outside catches her: crates or bottles being moved, dropped, or packed. Lucreta slowly lifts her head and retracts her arm from the wood. The sound leads her toward the window, but the deeper pull is the life outside: movement, streets, carriages, people, goods, sunlight, and ordinary public life.

Lucreta rises, not abruptly. She is controlled, but not fully steady. She gently moves past Vera, who says nothing and lowers her head. A small uneven step after standing hints that Lucreta is not fully right without turning the moment into an obvious symptom reveal.

At the window, Lucreta sees the street first: ordinary life continuing beyond the room. Vera then gently pulls her away. The street slips from view. Lucreta's eyes lift above the rooftops to the bright blue sky. The sky remains as the last piece of outside she can still hold. She closes her eyes. The sky lingers for a tiny fraction after she is pulled away, then leads into the mirror reveal: Lucreta's face finally seen in reflection, centered, eyes closed.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: stillness, restraint, ceremonial pressure.
- Shift: ordinary life outside pulls Lucreta away from the ritual.
- End emotion: the outside is taken away, leaving only the sky and then the mirror.
- Reader/viewer should feel: Lucreta is drawn toward a life she cannot enter.

Character Function:
- Lucreta:
  - Introduced through posture, hands, attention, restraint, and delayed face reveal.
  - Her attraction to the window is not childish distraction. It is hunger for ordinary life.
  - The small misstep after standing quietly suggests physical wrongness without explaining it.
- Vera:
  - Performs the ritual role without speaking in this beat.
  - Her silence and lowered head should feel controlled, trained, and socially constrained.

Structural Function:
- Establishes this scene's visual sequence: withheld face, flower crown, carved wood, window, outside life, sky, mirror.
- Creates the first movement from ritual room toward ordinary life, then back into ceremonial control.
- Prepares the mirror reveal and later mirror fixation.

Visual / Camera Function:
- Begin behind Lucreta, withholding her face.
- Use tactile focus on her hand and the carved bedframe before the window pull.
- Let the window view show ordinary life first, not only sky.
- Do not reveal Lucreta's face directly at the window.
- Let the street disappear first when Vera pulls her away.
- Let the sky remain briefly as the last outside image.
- Reveal Lucreta's face through the mirror, eyes closed.

Action Flow:
- F01 — Scene opens behind Lucreta at the foot of the bed.
- F02 — Lucreta's hand finds the carved wood.
- F03 — Ordinary life outside catches Lucreta's attention.
- F04 — Lucreta rises and moves past Vera.
- F05 — Lucreta reaches the window.
- F06 — Vera gently pulls Lucreta away.
- F07 — The street slips from view and the sky remains.
- F08 — The sky linger leads into the mirror reveal.

Key Images:
- Lucreta seen from behind, face withheld.
- Vera placing the flower crown.
- Lucreta's finger sliding through carved wood grooves.
- Sound outside pulling her attention.
- Small misstep after standing.
- Vera lowering her head without speaking.
- Ordinary life outside the window.
- Street slipping away.
- Bright blue sky above rooftops.
- Mirror reveal, face reflected, eyes closed.

Important Notes:
- The window view must include ordinary life outside, not only sky.
- The sky matters because it remains after the human street life disappears from view.
- The face reveal should happen through reflection, not direct frontal framing.
- Vera's silence matters. She lowers her head instead of speaking.
