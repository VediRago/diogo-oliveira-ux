# CEM

*One of the applied methods behind [the repository's principles](../PRINCIPLES.md).*

## The Method

A capability claim is easy to write and hard to trust.

Most resumes and portfolios assert skill directly — "I am good at X" — and ask the reader to take that on faith.

CEM starts from a different rule: capability should be evidenced, not summarized.

---

## The Problem

Evidence and evaluation done by the same process contaminate each other.

If the person looking for proof of a skill is also the one deciding whether that proof counts, the search quietly bends toward whatever confirms the answer they wanted.

This is not dishonesty.

It's structural.

Open-ended evidence discovery and independent evidence evaluation have to stay separate, or the second one stops being independent.

---

## The Fix

CEM separates the two processes deliberately.

Evidence gets discovered on its own terms, without already knowing what conclusion it needs to support.

Evaluation happens afterward, against evidence that was already fixed before judgment started.

Neither step gets to see the other's assumptions in advance.

---

## Application

This shows up directly in CEM's own structure — a decimal-numbered architecture, not a narrative one.

`000_Method` defines the rules before any capability is discussed.

`100_Foundations` and `400_Model` hold what's been found and how it's organized.

`9900_Capture` and `History/` preserve the record of what was discovered, unedited by later conclusions.

The numbering isn't decoration.

It keeps discovery and evaluation from collapsing into the same document, the same way the method keeps them from collapsing into the same process.
